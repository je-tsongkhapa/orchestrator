# REFERENCE — AI Vending Machines (Superorganism)

Superorganism's slice of the AI-vending-machine standard (the demand side; sibling to `REFERENCE-so-business-systems.md` on the supply side). Same definition as the ServiceGrid list: a *destination* you bring a cheap on-hand input to and leave with **one finished deliverable**. Superorganism's domain is the **inward, self-facing half** — managing your own **records, property, estate, and homestead** (the inward end of the People fault line; the outward, business-facing machines are ServiceGrid's).

## The standard — 8 traits

1. **Cheap, on-hand input** — the buyer already has it; zero special capture/integration.
2. **One finished artifact out** — a self-contained doc/plan/dossier/sheet/pack you take and leave.
3. **No per-unit incumbent for the small buyer** — incumbents are nonexistent, expensive pros, tools-you-operate, or enterprise-only.
4. **Low liability** — you review and use it; not legal/medical/financial advice you'd be sued over.
5. **Horizontal** · 6. **The reasoning over messy input is the work** (not a wrapper) · 7. **High leverage** (the artifact unlocks real value).
8. **A real moat** — a domain-specific structure or trust/verification layer, NOT raw generation. The 3 moat-types: **decode/cross-reference table · criteria/compliance matrix · reconciliation/closure tie-out** — every machine can cite or check its own output.
