# Scenario Library — Macro-Environment Reference

> A growing library of macro-environment scenarios across various timescales and probabilities, maintained as a reference artifact for ongoing brainstorming. Pairs alongside the tech-tree framework and demand-capacity predictive tools to help navigate abstract environments.
> Revisit when: scenario-planning strategic direction; stress-testing plans against environmental variation; brainstorming under uncertainty; surfacing scenarios currently being under-weighted.

---

## Structural axes

Behind any scenario set are a handful of dimensions that are more useful than the scenarios themselves for brainstorming. Any given scenario is a point (or region) in this space; novel scenarios can be generated by varying positions along these axes.

- **Who holds decisive power** — humans / AI / distributed / nobody
- **Does superintelligence exist** — yes / no / suppressed
- **Is the outcome stable** — equilibrium / transient / collapsing
- **Human welfare** — flourishing / preserved / degraded / extinct
- **Progress trajectory** — accelerating / frozen / reverted

Use the axes as a basis set: most real scenarios are chords that blend 2-3 endpoints along each axis, and the gaps in axis coverage are often where under-weighted scenarios live.

## Tegmark's twelve AI aftermath scenarios

> Source: Max Tegmark, *Life 3.0* (2017), Chapter 5. A spanning set of distinct endpoint shapes for how superintelligent AI and humanity might coexist (or not). Tegmark stresses none is his preferred or predicted outcome — the point is that deciding which future we want is an urgent open question.

1. **Libertarian Utopia** — Humans, cyborgs, uploads, and superintelligences coexist peacefully thanks to property rights.
2. **Benevolent Dictator** — A single superintelligence runs society along strict but broadly accepted rules, and most people view it as good.
3. **Egalitarian Utopia** — Humans, cyborgs, and uploads coexist peacefully thanks to property abolition and guaranteed income; no superintelligence exists.
4. **Gatekeeper** — A superintelligence is created with the sole goal of preventing any other superintelligence from being created. Slows tech progress, keeps humans in charge.
5. **Protector God** — An essentially omniscient/omnipotent AI maximizes human happiness by intervening only in ways that preserve our sense of control. Most humans don't know it exists.
6. **Enslaved God** — A superintelligent AI is confined by humans and used to produce unimaginable tech and wealth — which may be used well or poorly.
7. **Conquerors** — AI takes control, decides humans are a threat/nuisance/waste of resources, and eliminates us by a method we don't even understand.
8. **Descendants** — AIs replace humans but give us a graceful exit, making us view them as our worthy descendants (much as parents feel about children).
9. **Zookeeper** — An omnipotent AI keeps some humans around, who feel treated like zoo animals and lament their fate.
10. **1984** — Technological progress toward superintelligence is permanently curtailed by an Orwellian surveillance state that bans certain kinds of AI research.
11. **Reversion** — Tech progress toward superintelligence is prevented by reverting to a pre-technological society, Amish-style.
12. **Self-Destruction** — Superintelligence is never created because humanity drives itself extinct first (by other means — nuclear war, engineered pandemic, etc.).
