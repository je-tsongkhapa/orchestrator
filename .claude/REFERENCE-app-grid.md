# REFERENCE — App Grid (4 domains × 4 archetypes)

This reference maps a 4×4 grid: four domains (ServiceGrid, the field-service-management business; Superorganism, the user's life-as-org; Dreamtime, the autonomous-agent orchestrator; Pilgrimage, games + worldbuilding) crossed with the four app archetypes cut by core-loop autonomy × value-discipline (Console = inert/manage, Studio = inert/create, Game = running/play, Sandbox = autonomous/inhabit). The DIAGONAL is each domain's home archetype — where the domain's center of gravity already sits and where an app is most natural and most often already built: ServiceGrid→Console (DispatchDeck; the flagship FSM SaaS), Superorganism→Console (the shipped SO life-dashboard), Dreamtime→Sandbox (The Hollow; a self-organizing agent world), and Pilgrimage→two homes, Game (Pilgrim) AND Sandbox (Vivarium), since a pilgrimage is both a played journey and an inhabited world. The OFF-DIAGONAL is the backlog: it's where you ask whether crossing a domain into a non-home archetype yields a genuinely NEW app or just a re-skin of the home one, and it's where forced symmetry hides. The grid's single sharpest structural finding is that Studio is the universal white-space: it is NOT a home archetype in ANY of the four domains (two are Console-homes, two are running-homes), so the entire Studio COLUMN is off-diagonal — and every Studio cell collapses to the same shape, an authoring instrument for one homogeneous artifact whose value is INSTRUMENTAL to a home cell (make the quote the FSM Console sends, the life-model the SO Console manages, the world-seed the Dreamtime Sandbox runs, the world-bible the Pilgrimage Game/Sandbox play). Studio is the grid's connective tissue, not a destination — the on-ramp that feeds the homes.

> **Layer note.** This is a **Superorganism strategic map** — the portfolio of apps across the four ecosystem domains, generated by crossing each domain with the **4 app archetypes** (defined in `org-docs/.claude/REFERENCE-app-archetypes.md`). The machines each app *houses* are the 43-machine catalog (`REFERENCE-vending-machines.md` + `REFERENCE-so-vending-machines.md`). Apps are the layer **above** vending machines: an app is an archetype loaded into a domain; it contains/spawns the machines.

## Top opportunities

The strongest genuinely-new apps (natural fit + real distinct value, not a re-skin of the home app):

- **Roost** — *Dreamtime × Console* — Strongest off-diagonal natural fit in the grid AND the highest-leverage build because the substrate is already half-there. The orchestrator dashboard (/runs, /analytics, ExecutionEngine + SSE, 101 persisted runs, calibration hierarchy) is a proto-Console that manages ONE lineage at a time as a flat list; Roost is the upgrade to a governed standing FLEET with the six resources made visible+controllable (People=agent roster/trust, Money=burn-down vs budget, Time=queues/clocks, Things=deliverable inventory, Information=traces, Trust=reliability ledger) plus Evaluate-mode (Score/TierList the roster). Distinct value, not a re-skin: it is the cockpit you sit in WHILE the world runs — the manage-face the Sandbox (The Hollow) structurally needs. Pattern: Console-for-an-autonomous-domain = a control-room. Lower build cost than any Sandbox cell, and it's the natural first step that makes the heavier Dreamtime build observable and steerable.
- **Pilgrim** — *Pilgrimage × Game* — A declared HOME with ZERO built running-loop coverage — the highest value-fit-to-build-gap ratio on the board. Guided Journey is flagged the single cleanest archetype↔domain match in the entire catalog (LM🧑8): a pilgrimage/journey IS literally a ruled, goal-directed loop with a mastery gradient, gated world-disclosure, owned consequential choices, and a real curtain. The existing /exploration surface is entirely Console-shaped (tier-lists), so the play-machinery (GM pacing/branching engine, progression ratchet, win/lose, a real ending) is greenfield but the value-form fit is native, not stretched. Seeds its journeys directly from the already-built Atlas tiers, so the existing Console feeds it for free.
- **Vivarium / The Hollow** — *Pilgrimage + Dreamtime × Sandbox* — Listed as one opportunity because it IS one build: Vivarium (Pilgrimage Sandbox, the domain's 'world to inhabit') and The Hollow (Dreamtime Sandbox, the agent society that self-organizes) are the same engine — sovereign tick, finite commons, append-only chronicle, multi-agent cognition, catch-up digest, containment kill-switch — pointed at two different home domains. Both are NATURAL fit (all seven Sandbox value-forms map with no stretch) and both are 0%-built, and Emergent Order & Society is the one value-form NO other archetype produces (it's the ecosystem's signature exported capability: agents self-organize with no human architect). This is the grid's biggest build-multiplier: one heavy engine satisfies two named home cells. Flagged honestly as the most expensive cell(s) to stand up — build AFTER Roost gives you the glass to watch it through.
- **EstimateStudio** — *ServiceGrid × Studio* — The ONLY Studio cell whose artifact has terminal standalone value — a customer literally pays for the estimate — where LifeForge/Genesis Bench/Worldwright all author meta-documents whose worth is deferred to a home cell. Genuine single homogeneous artifact (the priced proposal) in a genuine live instrument (the flat-rate price-book canvas), with the maker unmistakably in an act-see-adjust loop: drag a line item, the proposal re-totals/re-prices/re-renders live; branch good/better/best variants risk-free; copilot drafts lines from job photos through the maker's own verbs. Six of Studio's seven forms land cleanly. Sits adjacent to a real revenue surface and to DispatchDeck's job cards, so it compounds the FSM Console rather than standing alone — the rare Studio that pays for itself.
- **ServiceSim** — *ServiceGrid × Game* — A real, well-motivated Game ONE ring out from the core work — and the domain itself flags the exact gap (B11 Training/LMS is shipping FAQ content with NO user-side JTBDs, marked 'supply without demand'). A diagnostic-and-repair training game (no-cool HVAC, slab leak) where the opponent is a believable equipment-fault model with fog-of-war over the hidden fault, played under scarcity (parts budget, callback clock, safety lockout). Worth building specifically because 'safe reps before real liability' is genuinely priced in skilled trades (callback costs, safety stakes) — the place a played loop delivers what a Console can't. Honest discount baked into the ranking: it's adjacent (how techs get READY, not the job itself), the readiness-number half folds out to Console Score, and a per-trade fault simulator is heavy — which is why it sits 5th, not higher.

## The read

The grid splits cleanly into three reads. (1) The strongest genuinely-NEW apps are the off-diagonal-or-greenfield cells that are NATURAL fit AND carry value distinct from the home app — not a re-presetting of it. The standout is Roost (Dreamtime×Console): an agent fleet is structurally a six-resource stateful domain (People=agents, Money=spend, Time=queues, Things=deliverables, Information=traces, Trust=reliability), the orchestrator already half-builds the surface, and a control-room over a RUNNING world is genuinely distinct from the world itself — it's the glass, not the terrarium. The pattern here: Console-for-an-autonomous-domain = a control-tower/mission-control, the inert manage-face every Sandbox needs, and it's the cleanest off-diagonal natural fit in the whole grid. Next are the two diagonal-but-greenfield homes in Pilgrimage: Pilgrim (Game) — Guided Journey is flagged the single best archetype↔domain match in the catalog (LM🧑8), a pilgrimage IS a ruled, gated, mastery-curved journey to a curtain — and Vivarium (Sandbox), the literal 'world to inhabit.' Crucial leverage note: Vivarium (Pilgrimage Sandbox) and The Hollow (Dreamtime Sandbox) are the SAME dreamtime engine (sovereign tick, commons ledger, catch-up digest, kill-switch, multi-agent cognition) pointed at two domains — build one and you ~80% build the other; this is the grid's biggest build-multiplier. The one off-diagonal Studio worth real money is EstimateStudio (ServiceGrid×Studio): alone among the four Studio cells its artifact is TERMINAL-valued (a customer literally pays for the estimate), whereas LifeForge / Genesis Bench / Worldwright author meta-documents whose value is deferred to a home cell. (2) The forced cells cluster on one axis: AUTONOMY-into-an-autonomy-hostile-or-autonomy-inverted domain. Both Sandbox-into-Console-domain cells (ServiceGrid×Sandbox, Superorganism×Sandbox) fail the same way — a single principal's small fixed set of functionaries has no society to emerge, so four of Sandbox's seven forms (Liveness, Accreted History, Emergent Order, Commons) go vestigial and what's left ('work runs while you're away') is just OvernightGuild, an agent-crew over a Console, NOT an inhabited world. Symmetrically, Dreamtime×Game (Wager Before Dawn) inverts the domain against itself: Dreamtime's premise is ABSENCE, Game's premise is a PRESENT human at the controls, and the only seam between them is spectator-betting, which is thin. (3) A cross-cutting TELL runs through every Game cell: the scored pole always leaks out to Console Score. In ServiceGrid×Game (tech-readiness number), Superorganism×Game (season placement), and Dreamtime×Game (forecasting signal), the 'assessment half' is really a Console scorer wearing Game clothes — the genuinely-Game residue is always the LIVED loop, never the measured one. This makes Game-for-a-Console-domain (life-gamification, training-as-game) systematically THINNER than it first looks: you must impose a finite SEASON on an infinite domain, the opponent degrades to a passive past-self (real adversity only appears in co-op/AI-ally variants), and Inhabited-World/Emergent-Depth don't transfer because the 'mechanics' are real behaviors, not an authored rule-system. Net: the diagonal is mostly built or naturally buildable; the real new-build leverage is Roost + the shared Pilgrimage/Dreamtime Sandbox engine + EstimateStudio; the rest of the off-diagonal is either thin (Game column over Console-domains) or forced (Sandbox over Console-domains, Game over Dreamtime).

**Forced / redundant cells** (flagged so symmetry isn't mistaken for opportunity):

- ServiceGrid × Sandbox (TerritoryTwin): FORCED — field service is the single most autonomy-hostile spot in the grid; you cannot let agents dispatch real crews to real customers unattended, because the liability and physical-world accountability that DEFINE the domain are exactly what 'dreamtime, hands-off-the-wheel' violates. The only non-absurd version is a SIMULATED market for planning, which quietly converts the value back into Console/Studio what-if evaluation (a scenario engine you read off, not a world you inhabit). Tell: of Sandbox's 7 native forms only Stewardship Leverage + Dreamtime Yield carry weight; the four that make a Sandbox worth inhabiting — Liveness, Accreted History, Emergent Order & Society, Commons Stock — are vestigial (nobody treasures the lore of a fake HVAC market). The honest residue (OvernightGuild on a real back-office backlog) is a useful daemon but is the proof, not a refutation, that Sandbox-AS-WORLD is forced here.
- Superorganism × Sandbox (The Overnight Household): FORCED — same failure as ServiceGrid×Sandbox, for the same structural reason. A single-user inward life-org has no SOCIETY to emerge: the 'agents' are a fixed, small set of functionaries serving ONE principal under explicit policy, so there is no population-scale emergence, no norms/markets/reputations forming, and no surprise you'd even WANT (you do not want your finances evolving unauthored behavior). Only the autonomy axis transfers (Dreamtime Yield + Stewardship Leverage), which makes this an autonomous-agent-CREW over a Console, not a living world — i.e. 'OvernightGuild aimed inward.' The overnight life-admin council is genuinely worth building; calling it a Sandbox-world is the dpress-up. Pattern across both forced Sandbox cells: Sandbox-into-a-Console-domain always degrades to OvernightGuild.
- Dreamtime × Game (Wager Before Dawn): FORCED — the domain's defining premise is ABSENCE (value scaling with dreamtime elapsed, the loop running while you sleep); a Game's defining premise is the exact inverse (a present human at the controls, a tight interactive loop, a curtain you play TO). To make a Game here you must invert the domain against itself — seal the away-world, fast-forward at watch-speed, and bolt a wager/scoring frame on. It technically works (forecasting emergence is a real deep skill) but the agency is hollow: you don't PLAY the world, you bet on it and watch, so Inhabited World, Moment Texture, and Social Stakes don't land, and the scored pole (your forecast accuracy) folds out to Console Score anyway. The only seam between present-tense play and an absence-yield domain is spectator-betting, which is thin.
- Pilgrimage × Console (Cartographer's Atlas): NOT forced, but flag as REDUNDANT-AS-A-NEW-APP — it earns its seat ONLY because it already exists. It is the entire current /exploration surface (Atlas tier-lists + Survey coverage), so there is no new app to build here; listing it as an opportunity would double-count shipped work. It's an honest STRETCH (Console manages the meta-layer of exploration — the registry and rankings — not the play itself; you do not pilgrimage by reading a dashboard), defensible only as the already-real capture/ranking substrate that FEEDS the two home cells (seeds Pilgrim's journeys, supplies Worldwright's raw material). Useful, built, but not a new opportunity.
- Studio column, general caveat (LifeForge / Genesis Bench / Worldwright): all three are honest STRETCHES, not forced — each authors a genuine single homogeneous artifact (life operating-model / world-seed / world-bible) in a real direct-manipulation + dry-run loop. But their value is INSTRUMENTAL, never terminal: the artifact is a meta-document whose worth is realized later in a home cell (editing the life-model doesn't move the money; a world-seed has near-zero standalone value until the Sandbox runs it). They are worth building as on-ramps to the homes, NOT as destinations — and they rank below EstimateStudio precisely because EstimateStudio's artifact is the one a customer pays for directly. Liveness/inhabited-presence cannot be felt at any of these benches (the thing isn't alive yet), so Studio's two richest experiential forms are thin across the whole column.

## The 16 cells

### ServiceGrid  ·  *home: Console*

**Console — DispatchDeck — the service-business operations command center**  ·  🟢 *natural*
- A single live operations board for an SMB service company: every open job is a card on a time × crew grid, and from that one surface the operator sees and steers all six resources at once — Time (the schedule/dispatch board + route ETAs), People (which tech, which customer, the comms thread), Things (truck stock + the part the job needs), Money (the job's quote→invoice→paid state), Trust (license/permit/insurance gating who can take the job), Information (job photos, the customer-asset service history). Core loop: a job/lead lands → operator (or the AI dispatcher as an invoked tool) assigns it to the right tech on the right slot → drags to re-balance the day → the board re-derives routes, ETAs, capacity load, and margin live → invoice fires on completion → reconcile. It folds in Evaluate-mode natively: Score a tech's readiness for a job (cert + skill + stock), and TierList today's jobs by margin × urgency × SLA-risk to decide what gets crewed first.
- *value-forms:* Time — the dispatch board/route/capacity is the spine; the schedule made visible and draggable IS the product · People — crews + customers made visible+controllable (who's assigned, the unified comms thread, who's owed a callback) · Things — truck stock + the specific part a job needs, surfaced against the job · Money — per-job quote→invoice→paid state and live margin made visible on the card · Trust — license/permit/insurance/cert state gating who may legally take a job (Evaluate as a guardrail) · Information — job photos, customer-asset registry, service history catalogued against the account · Evaluate/curate — Score (tech-readiness-against-a-job standard) + TierList (rank today's jobs into crew-now / queue / defer tiers)
- *machines it houses:* Score — tech-readiness-against-a-job (cert/skill/truck-stock as the calibrated standard); also customer-credit-risk and job-margin scoring · TierList — rank today's job queue into defended crew-now / queue / defer tiers (the daily triage map) · SitePlan — a Console machine that lives natively here (address + phone photos → to-scale plan with setbacks) for fence/landscape/pool jobs at quote time · SpecLift — photos of installed equipment → serial-decoded spec sheet, feeding the customer-asset registry (C6) · PartFinder — photo of a broken part → OEM/cross-ref + sourcing dossier, called from the job card by the field tech · ReconLedger — statement PDFs → reconciled books, the Money-resource back-office tie-out · RepairCard — a repair video → service report + fault-keyed SOP, generated off completed-job media
- *current state:* EXISTS, and is the domain's whole reason for being. servicegrid-app is the flagship FSM SaaS (42 systems across all 6 resources — dispatch board E1, route opt E2, payments A1, price book D5, customer-asset registry C6, etc.). The org also runs its own Console (the 55-system founder dashboard). Console is not a cell to invent here — it is the load-bearing center. The design question is which SHARPEST Console blade to name, not whether one fits.
- *why this fit:* This is the textbook Console: an inert, operator-driven control surface over a stateful, real-money, multi-resource domain where the agent is an invoked tool (the AI dispatcher proposes; the human steers). Field service is structurally a 6-resource stewardship problem — jobs, crews, trucks, parts, money, schedules, compliance — and dispatch is the one board the whole business hangs off (E1 is the system every other FSM system references). It delivers all six resources made visible+controllable plus both Evaluate modes, the full Console taxonomy, with zero forcing. The only honest caveat is incumbency, not fit: the operations resources have strong incumbents (ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro), so the durable edge sits in the Evaluate-mode fold — the readiness-Score and job-TierList standards are the moat that ports, exactly as the Console reference predicts.

**Studio — EstimateStudio — the live quote/proposal instrument (price-book-backed)**  ·  🟢 *natural*
- A performative canvas for building one homogeneous artifact: the customer estimate/proposal. The maker (owner or salesperson, often standing in the customer's driveway on a tablet) assembles the quote line-by-line from the flat-rate price book — drag a line item in, tune quantity/labor/markup with direct-manipulation knobs, and the artifact re-totals, re-prices, and re-renders the customer-facing proposal instantly (closed-loop liveness). The deeper instrument underneath is the price-book/flat-rate-menu AUTHORING tool — the owner crafts the reusable catalog of priced services once, and every estimate is played out of it. Core loop: open a job → copilot drafts good/better/best line items from the job photos + customer-asset history → maker direct-manipulates the lines, branches a 'what-if we add the membership' variant (reversible exploration), auditions the three tiers side-by-side → snaps to a clean, on-brand, correctly-totaled proposal → exports to a signable PDF where preview == the signed artifact. Craft compounds: a tuned line-item bundle ('AC install + permit + haul-away') is saved as a reusable rack pulled into the next estimate.
- *value-forms:* Closed-Loop Liveness — every line edit re-totals, re-prices, and re-renders the proposal within the felt-delay window; the maker performs the quote rather than filling a form · Direct-Manipulation Fidelity — you grab the artifact's own primitive (a line item, a markup %, a tier toggle), not a command language; nudge one number, the whole proposal responds · Reversible Exploration — branch good/better/best variants and 'add the service plan' what-ifs at zero risk; audition them against the running total; keep one, abandon the rest · Convergent Artifact Integrity — the proposal stays internally consistent (totals tie out, tax/permit lines correct-by-rule, on-brand) and is taken through the last-10% finish to a signable, exportable deliverable · Embedded Co-Authorship — the copilot drafts and varies line items through the SAME verbs the maker uses (insert-line, swap-tier, apply-markup); every suggestion lands as a tunable, revertible edit, not an answer to accept whole · Reusable Craft Capital — the dispensable residue: saved priced line-item racks/bundles and proposal scaffolds (the price book itself is the maker's accumulating instrument library) that lower the floor for every future quote
- *machines it houses:* StarterKit — the price-book-backed fill-in proposal scaffold, correct-by-construction (the Scaffold class; SG's named Studio machine) · AssetGenerator — brief + brand → the finished, export-ready customer proposal/marketing leave-behind (the Whole class) · Brandify — apply the service company's logo/palette/voice so every estimate and proposal renders on-brand (the Modifier class; SG's named Studio machine)
- *current state:* PARTIAL. sg-studio exists as a HEADLESS content-slicing capability node (/org, /product, /public slice APIs) — it composes/extracts content artifacts, so it is a real Studio ACT, but it has no live performative instrument (no transport, no direct-manipulation surface, no maker-in-the-loop). It's the create-pole present in skeleton form. The richer, genuinely-Studio-shaped artifact in the FSM domain is unbuilt: the priced estimate/proposal authored on top of the flat-rate price book (D5 + D3 eSignature + D6 measurement tools).
- *why this fit:* Leans natural because there is a genuine single homogeneous artifact (the estimate) made in a genuine live instrument (the price-book canvas), and the maker is unmistakably inside an act-see-adjust loop — this is not Console's 'observe a settled system,' it's 'perform a thing into being.' Six of Studio's seven value-forms land cleanly. The honest pull toward stretch: (a) the FSM estimate is a constrained, low-ceiling artifact compared to a DAW track or a Figma file — expressive depth/mastery is shallow (a price quote has limited 'voice'), so that one value-form is thin; (b) the domain's incumbents bundle a forms/estimate builder, so the differentiated Studio surface is the live-instrument feel + the copilot-from-photos, not the existence of a quote tool. SG's own demand cut already names its three live Studio machines (AssetGenerator/Brandify/StarterKit) — EstimateStudio is the operations-native fourth, the price-book instance of the Scaffold + Whole classes.

**Game — ServiceSim — the diagnostic-and-job training game for field techs**  ·  🟡 *stretch*
- A ruled, goal-directed training game a technician PLAYS to build real field competence safely. The tech is dropped into a simulated job — a no-cool HVAC call, a tripping breaker, a slab leak — at a job-site/equipment they must diagnose and repair under constraints (a parts budget, a callback clock, a safety checklist). The opponent is the equipment-and-fault model: it fails believably, hides state behind readings the tech must probe (fog-of-war over the real fault), and reveals consequences only as they act (wrong diagnosis → callback; skipped lockout → safety strike). Core loop: read the symptom → probe/measure → form a hypothesis → commit a repair under scarcity → the sim resolves consequences → score, debrief, retry. A dynamic-difficulty director holds the tech in the flow channel — ratcheting fault complexity as win-rate rises — and a calibrated curriculum walks them from 'replace a capacitor' to 'intermittent multi-fault under time pressure.' The lived pole is the training; the SCORED pole feeds a readiness Score back into DispatchDeck (this tech is now certified-by-evidence on heat-pump diagnostics), and post-callback the Console can prescribe the exact scenario that matches the real-world miss.
- *value-forms:* Mastery Gradient — the core: a calibrated-challenge loop that reads demonstrated diagnostic skill and holds fault difficulty just above current ability, banking transferable trade competence plus the legible evidence (a readiness score) that proves it · Decision Weight — a diagnosis/repair under scarcity (limited parts budget, a ticking callback clock, irreversible safety choices) where the wrong call has a felt, hard-to-undo consequence — the same stakes that make real field decisions matter, made safe to fail at · Inhabited World — being-a-tech in a coherent simulated job-site/shop you progressively understand, with hidden fault state disclosed only through probing (the discovered map of how this equipment actually fails) · Emergent Depth — a few orthogonal fault mechanics (electrical × refrigerant × airflow × control) compose into multi-fault scenarios no single case authored, giving genuine re-enterable diagnostic variety · Moment Texture — the read-probe-confirm rhythm and the satisfying click of a correct diagnosis (serves the lived play) · Instrumented Signal (scored pole) — the same loop pointed at measurement yields a same-yardstick competence placement; this is what folds to Console Score and gates real dispatch
- *machines it houses:* Guided Journey — the LIVED-play machine this app spawns: a calibrated, narrated run through a trade's diagnostic curriculum (Mastery Gradient + Inhabited World) · Experience (agent-native) — the same scenario engine sold to an FSM agent that wants reps on a novel diagnostic task before doing it for real (the one place an agent occupies the played pole) · Score (folds to Console) — the scored pole: the readiness/competence assessment that grades supplied evidence and gates dispatch — built BY the game loop, called as a Console scorer
- *current state:* NONE as a played loop — but the demand-side gap is explicitly flagged. B11 (Training/LMS) is shipping trade-by-trade FAQ content with NO JTBDs framing the user-side experience (the jtbds doc marks it 🚨 'supply without demand,' with proposed-but-unmapped jobs like 'P3 looks up procedure during a visit' and 'P3 receives a learning prompt after a callback'). That unframed training/onboarding surface is the one seam in the domain where a played, ruled loop delivers value a Console can't.
- *why this fit:* Stretch, honestly. The domain's center of gravity is real operations, not play — you cannot make dispatching or invoicing a game without breaking the liability and accountability that define field service (a 'game' that rolls a real truck is malpractice). What IS legitimately playable is the ADJACENT training/onboarding surface, and the domain itself flags that exact gap (B11 unframed). So the Game cell is real and well-motivated, but it sits one ring out from the core work — it's how techs get READY to do the job, not the job. Two further honesty checks: (1) per the Game reference's scored-vs-lived split, the assessment half (the readiness number) is really Console Score wearing Game clothes — only the LIVED training loop is the Game proper; (2) building a believable equipment-fault simulator is heavy (real domain models per trade), which is why incumbents ship static FAQ/video LMS instead. Worth it only if 'safe reps before real liability' is priced as the value — which in skilled trades, with callback costs and safety stakes, it genuinely is.

**Sandbox — TerritoryTwin — the always-running simulated service-market (a SimCity for your service business)**  ·  🔴 *forced*
- A persistent digital-twin of a service company's market that runs on its own clock whether or not the owner is watching. Agent-techs, agent-customers (demand arriving seasonally — AC calls in July, furnace calls in January), and agent-competitors inhabit a model of the territory and act autonomously: jobs get booked, crews dispatched, reviews accrue, competitors poach, the simulated P&L moves — overnight, fast-forwarded. The human is the STEWARD/gardener, not the operator: you set standing rules (pricing policy, hiring triggers, service-area boundaries, on-call SLA), nudge one incentive ('cut membership price 10%'), or seed a condition ('a competitor opens across town'), then walk away and catch up on what the simulated business did. The payoff is the catch-up digest: 'your fast-forwarded Q3 — margin held but you lost 4 techs to burnout because the on-call rule overloaded weekends; the price cut won 60 members but cannibalized full-price installs.' Core loop: author a rule/nudge/seed → let dreamtime run → read the ranked catch-up of what emerged → adjust → re-run or fork a counterfactual ('what if I'd hired two more instead of cutting price'). Borrows OvernightGuild for the literal back-office-grind variant (let inhabitant agents chew a real backlog overnight and settle a deposit).
- *value-forms:* Stewardship Leverage — the load-bearing form: shape a whole self-running market through small well-placed interventions (a pricing tweak, a hiring rule, a seeded competitor) and read the simulated business's HONEST response — leverage + naturalistic measurement, the one Sandbox form that genuinely fits because planning-by-intervention is real value · Dreamtime Yield — fast-forward absence into a settled readout (a simulated quarter run overnight) or, in the OvernightGuild variant, a settled deposit of actually-harvested back-office work · Emergence & Surprise — second-order market dynamics no one scripted (a price cut cannibalizes installs; an SLA rule burns out crews) crystallizing from agent interactions — the reason to simulate rather than spreadsheet · Accreted History — a path-dependent record of the simulated territory's decisions you can read back and fork (thin: nobody treasures the lore of a fake HVAC market) · Emergent Order & Society — vestigial: agent-techs/customers form crude market equilibria, but no one wants a self-governing society of imaginary plumbers as a destination · Commons Stock & Continuity — vestigial: the territory's 'standing stock' is just the simulated P&L; it rides along in the catch-up rather than being inhabited
- *machines it houses:* OvernightGuild — the one honest fit: a backlog of specialized back-office FSM work (data cleanup, bulk estimate drafting, review triage, lead enrichment) + a wake time → a settled, attributed deposit of harvested work · TimeWarpKiln — fast-forward the simulated territory through a season to provoke the market-dynamics surprise cheaply · VivariumNudge — the stewardship surface: nudge one pricing/hiring/SLA rule on the running market-twin and read the honest propagated consequence · SocietyFork — fork a counterfactual territory ('what if I'd hired instead of discounted') and run both forward to compare
- *current state:* NONE, and structurally the hardest fit in the domain. The only foothold: OvernightGuild is SG's single named Sandbox machine (a backlog of specialized work + a wake time → a settled deposit of harvested work) — i.e., back-office grind run unattended, NOT a world to inhabit. The org has dreamtime infrastructure elsewhere, but nothing world-shaped exists for FSM, because field service is the antithesis of unattended autonomy: every tick is a real truck, a real customer, real liability.
- *why this fit:* Forced, and I'd rather say so than dress it up. **Game — Arena of the Self — the Self-Improvement Season**  ·  🟡 *stretch*
- A bounded, ruled PLAY loop where your life metrics are the game-state and the opponent is your own decaying baseline (entropy / past self). The core loop is a SEASON: at season-start the engine snapshots your baseline and sets a calibrated challenge bar just above current ability (per Mastery Gradient), each day/week is a turn where real behavior (rituals kept, deep-work hours, deals advanced, streaks held) scores against the bar, the difficulty ratchets as you succeed, failure is cheap and informative (data, not shame), and the season ENDS at a curtain (a graded run you can't re-scum) with a legible result you bank — then a new, harder season starts. Co-op variant: team with AI allies (a coach-agent, a rival-agent) who share the goal. The fun is the calibrated-challenge gradient + the decision weight of where to spend a scarce day + watchable self-rivalry across seasons.
- *value-forms:* Mastery Gradient (the engine reads demonstrated adherence/skill and tunes the live bar to hold you in the flow channel, ratcheting as you succeed — the banked competence + honest contingent progress markers; this is the core fit, and it's exactly what Dinacharya's decay model and the Skill Tree DAG gesture at) · Decision Weight (a season makes each day a consequential, hard-to-undo choice under scarcity — where you spend the one non-renewable resource; no costless undo because the season is binding/permadeath-flavored, so the road-not-taken is felt) · Social Stakes (self-rivalry across remembered seasons mints a prestige/standing arc against your own past; Co-op with AI allies adds coupled-fate fellowship — the only multi-agent stake an inward domain naturally supports) · Moment Texture (juice/cadence on the daily turn — streak feedback, the bounded gently-paced loop of a daily check-in; the cozy/submission pole that Dinacharya already lives in) · Instrumented Signal (your own season runs are seedable, comparable placements + a behavioral corpus of your adherence — the scored pole, which per the Game arc actually folds OUT to Console Score, i.e. the assessment is a Console call, not the Game itself)
- *machines it houses:* Co-op (credit + a mission/scenario -> one co-op run teaming with AI allies to a shared goal — a coach-agent + rival-agent season toward a self-improvement target) · Guided Journey (credit + a journey setup + personal seeds -> a gamemaster-run solo journey to an ending — a narrated, calibrated transformation arc with a real curtain over your own metrics) · Lockbox (credit + a setup -> a fair self-contained solvable you crack — a bounded decision-weight puzzle seeded from a real life-tradeoff, with a proven single solution)
- *current state:* PARTIAL — gamified surfaces exist, no bounded Game-with-a-curtain. Dinacharya is a shipped don't-break-the-chain habit game (streaks, spaced-repetition strength decay, deep-work timer, habit-sandbox graduation). No matchmaking, no scored season, no AI opponent over life-state.
- *why this fit:* Genuine stretch, leaning playable. A real Game needs a ruled loop, a fail-state, an opponent, and a CURTAIN — and self-improvement is intrinsically open-ended and infinite (life has no win screen; the org mission is the infinite game). The honest fix is to impose a FINITE game on top: a bounded SEASON with a snapshot baseline, a binding end, and your-past-self as the opponent. That delivers Mastery Gradient and Decision Weight authentically. What does NOT transfer: Inhabited World (no fiction to discover — it's your real life), Emergent Depth (few composable rules; the 'mechanics' are real behaviors, not an authored system that yields unauthored strategy surplus), and a true adversary (your baseline is a passive opponent — Social Stakes only really fires in the Co-op/AI-ally variant). The catalog's own finding reinforces the stretch: the *scored* pole of life-as-game is an assessment that folds to Console Score, so the residue that is genuinely a Game is the LIVED season — playable, but a thinner Game than a designed one.

### Dreamtime  ·  *home: Sandbox*

**Studio — Genesis Bench**  ·  🟡 *stretch*

**Game — Wager Before Dawn**  ·  🔴 *forced*
- A present-tense game played AGAINST the dreamtime — you bet on what the autonomous world will do, then it runs to a curtain and scores you. The core loop is predict -> commit -> watch-the-fast-forward -> reveal -> score: before a dreamtime window opens, you read the world's current state and stake a forecast (which faction wins the feud, whether the commons survives, which agent gets richest, what emergent strategy spreads). You lock it in, the Time-Warp governor fast-forwards the sealed world to the curtain at watch-speed, and the result scores your read of the system. Mastery is learning to forecast emergence; the opponent is not an agent but the irreducible surprise of many agents compounding. A speed-run variant inverts it: race a clock to hand-steer a colony to a target state before the autonomous agents would have reached it on their own.
- *value-forms:* Decision Weight — your forecast is a single high-consequence committed bet that the world then adjudicates; the weight comes from irreversibility plus genuine uncertainty about an emergent outcome · Instrumented Signal — every match is a scored, logged read of how well you predict the system; over many rounds it becomes a clean skill-signal (this doubles as an honest eval of forecasting emergence) · Mastery Gradient — you get measurably better at reading which local conditions tip into which macro-outcomes; the gradient is the forecasting skill itself · Emergent Depth — the thing you bet on is genuinely emergent (no script to memorize), so the strategy space never bottoms out — the same depth that makes the Sandbox surprising makes the bet deep
- *machines it houses:* Lockbox (commit-and-reveal: seal your forecast, fast-forward the world, reveal+score against the locked prediction — the literal predict->reveal mechanic) · Guided Journey (a scripted ladder of forecasting challenges of rising difficulty, teaching you to read emergence)
- *current state:* None, and structurally counter-grain. Nothing in the orchestrator is built to be PLAYED to a curtain while present. The only adjacent surface is the OODA-clock replay, which is spectation of a finished run, not a played match. The whole substrate is tuned for absence (SSE streams you check asynchronously, Slack notifications for when you're jogging/driving, settle-on-a-wake-time) — the opposite of a game's demand that you be present at the controls.
- *why this fit:* The domain's defining premise is ABSENCE — value scaling with dreamtime elapsed, not human attention; the loop running WHILE YOU SLEEP. A Game's defining premise is the exact opposite: a present human at the controls, a tight interactive loop, a curtain you play TO. To make a Game here you must invert the domain against itself — convert the away-world into a present-tense spectacle by sealing it, fast-forwarding at watch-speed, and bolting a wager/scoring frame on. It technically works (forecasting emergence is a real, deep skill and the Time-Warp governor + sealed-fork already exist to power it), but the agency is hollow: you don't play the world, you bet on it and watch — the moment-to-moment texture, inhabited-world feeling, and social stakes that make Games sing are mostly absent, and several of Game's seven value-forms (Inhabited World, Moment Texture, Social Stakes) don't land. A forced cell, honestly labeled: the present-tense game and the absence-yield domain pull in opposite directions, and the only seam between them is spectator-betting, which is thin.

**Sandbox — The Hollow**  ·  🟢 *natural*
- A persistent multi-agent world your fleet INHABITS and self-organizes within while you are away — the full Sandbox. A standing population of agents lives in a shared world with a finite commons, a sovereign tick that advances on the world's own clock whether or not you're watching, and a constitution you seeded. They perceive locally, speak, contend over resources, form roles and reputations, trade, ally, and feud — and out of that, a society and a body of work crystallize that no one scripted. The core loop is the steward's catch-up loop: leave -> the world runs unattended (fast-forwarded), agents produce + organize + accrete history -> RETURN to a ranked, causal catch-up digest ('the granary emptied BECAUSE the road-agents struck') -> spend scarce attention on the few high-gain seams (seed a rule, nudge one agent, ratify a norm, fork a branch) -> leave again.
